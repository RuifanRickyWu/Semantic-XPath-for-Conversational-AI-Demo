10-Day Japan Trip  Quick Overview

A practical 10-day route from Tokyo to Miyajima balancing major sights, culture, food, and comfortable travel pacing.

Trip Flow Summary
Start in Tokyo, enjoy a Hakone nature/onsen stop, then immerse in Kyoto culture before finishing with Osaka and Hiroshima/Miyajima.
- Days 1-3: Tokyo highlights and Hakone day/overnight
- Days 4-6: Kyoto and Nara cultural core
- Days 7-9: Osaka urban food scene then Hiroshima/Miyajima

Daily Pacing Advice
Mix busy sightseeing days with lighter travel or onsen/rest days to avoid burnout.
- Plan mornings for top sites to avoid crowds
- Reserve evenings for relaxed dining or neighborhood walks
- Include one full rest or optional activity day (e.g., Universal Studios, Hakone onsen)

Transport and Tickets
Use JR Pass for long-distance shinkansen legs and buy local tickets or IC card for city transit.
- Consider a 7-day JR Pass activated for Kyoto-to-Hiroshima leg
- Get a Suica/Pasmo or Icoca IC card for city trains and buses
- Reserve shinkansen seats for peak travel days

Accommodation Tips
Stay near major stations for convenience and experience at least one ryokan/onsen overnight.
- Tokyo: Shinjuku/Shibuya for connectivity
- Kyoto: Central Kawaramachi/Gion for walking access
- Try a ryokan in Hakone or Miyajima for traditional lodging

Food & Cultural Notes
Sample regional specialties and be mindful of local etiquette in temples, shrines, and public transport.
- Try takoyaki and okonomiyaki in Osaka, oysters in Miyajima
- Remove shoes where required and keep phone conversations low on trains
- Carry cash for small vendors; many places accept cards but not all

Final Departure Planning
Build buffer time for transfers and last-minute packing before your international flight.
- Allow 3+ hours for airport transfer if departing same day as travel
- Check luggage forwarding services if moving between many cities
- Confirm flight check-in and customs timing the day before departure
